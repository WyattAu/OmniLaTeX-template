\begin{abstract}
This paper presents a novel approach to distributed consensus that combines
the strengths of existing protocols while addressing their individual
limitations.  Our hybrid protocol achieves stronger consistency guarantees
while maintaining high availability, demonstrating significant improvements
over state-of-the-art methods on benchmark workloads.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Distributed systems are fundamental to modern cloud computing infrastructure.
The challenge of maintaining consistency while ensuring availability remains
a critical research problem~\cite{bibid}.  This paper addresses this
challenge through a novel hybrid consensus protocol.

\section{Background}
The CAP theorem establishes fundamental limits on distributed system
design.  Existing protocols such as Paxos and Raft provide different
trade-offs between consistency and availability.

\section{Proposed Method}
Our approach combines the leader-based efficiency of Raft with the
multi-decree optimisation of Paxos.  The key insight is that under
normal operation, a single leader can process commits efficiently,
while under failure conditions, the protocol degrades gracefully to
a multi-decree consensus.

\section{Evaluation}
We evaluate our protocol on a 3-node cluster with synthetic workloads
measuring throughput, latency, and fault recovery time.

\section{Conclusion}
The proposed hybrid protocol demonstrates that it is possible to achieve
stronger consistency guarantees without sacrificing availability under
normal operation.
