\section*{Preamble}

WHEREAS, the City of Oakridge has identified critical deficiencies in its
aging transportation, water, and telecommunications infrastructure; and

WHEREAS, the Federal Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act of 2021 provides
matching grants for municipalities that adopt comprehensive modernization
plans; and

WHEREAS, the City Council has determined that coordinated investment in
infrastructure modernization will promote public safety, economic growth,
and environmental sustainability;

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT ORDAINED by the City Council of Oakridge as follows:

\section{Title and Effective Date}

\begin{description}
    \item[Title.] This ordinance shall be known and may be cited as the
        ``Municipal Infrastructure Modernization Act of 2026'' (Ordinance
        No.\ 2026-047).
    \item[Effective Date.] This ordinance shall take effect on August 1,
        2026, unless otherwise specified in Section~7 hereof.
    \item[Severability.] If any provision of this ordinance is held invalid
        by a court of competent jurisdiction, the remaining provisions shall
        continue in full force and effect.
\end{description}

\section{Definitions}

For the purposes of this ordinance, the following definitions apply:

\begin{description}
    \item[``City''] means the City of Oakridge, including all departments,
        agencies, and instrumentalities thereof.
    \item[``Council''] means the City Council of Oakridge.
    \item[``Infrastructure''] means all publicly owned transportation,
        water, wastewater, stormwater, telecommunications, and energy
        systems.
    \item[``Modernization Plan''] means the comprehensive infrastructure
        improvement program adopted pursuant to Section~3 of this ordinance.
    \item[``Project''] means any individual construction, repair, or
        upgrade activity undertaken pursuant to the Modernization Plan.
    \item[``Director''] means the Director of Public Works or designee.
    \item[``Federal Grant''] means any funds received from the United States
        government under the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act or
        successor legislation.
\end{description}

\section{Modernization Plan}

\subsection{Adoption}

The Director of Public Works shall, within 120 days of the effective date
of this ordinance, prepare and submit to the Council a comprehensive
Modernization Plan that addresses the following priority areas:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Transportation Infrastructure.} Repair or replacement of
        bridges, roads, and intersections rated below acceptable condition,
        including the integration of smart traffic management systems.
    \item \textbf{Water and Wastewater Systems.} Upgrade of aging pipes,
        treatment facilities, and pumping stations to meet current
        Environmental Protection Agency standards.
    \item \textbf{Stormwater Management.} Construction of green
        infrastructure to reduce combined sewer overflows and mitigate
        flood risk in designated floodplains.
    \item \textbf{Broadband Access.} Deployment of municipal fiber-optic
        networks to underserved areas to achieve citywide broadband
        connectivity.
    \item \textbf{Energy Resilience.} Installation of microgrids and
        backup power systems at critical facilities, including hospitals,
        schools, and emergency services.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Public Review}

The draft Modernization Plan shall be made available for public review for
a period of not fewer than 30 days. The Director shall hold at least two
public hearings before submitting the final Plan to the Council.

\section{Funding}

\subsection{Funding Sources}

The Modernization Plan shall be funded through the following sources:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Federal matching grants secured under the Infrastructure
        Investment and Jobs Act.
    \item Municipal bonds issued pursuant to the Oakridge Municipal Bond
        Act (Chapter 12 of the City Code).
    \item State revolving loan funds for water and wastewater projects.
    \item Dedicated revenue from the Infrastructure Improvement Surcharge
        established by Ordinance No.\ 2025-031.
\end{itemize}

\section{Oversight and Reporting}

\subsection{Quarterly Reports}

The Director shall submit quarterly progress reports to the Council
beginning on October 1, 2026. Each report shall include:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Status of each Project, including percentage completion.
    \item Expenditures to date against the approved budget.
    \item Any delays, cost overruns, or changes in scope.
    \item Updated projections for remaining Project timelines.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Independent Audit}

The Council shall contract with an independent certified public accounting
firm to conduct an annual audit of all expenditures under this ordinance.
The audit report shall be made available to the public within 60 days of
the fiscal year close.

\section{Penalties for Non-Compliance}

Any contractor or subcontractor that fails to comply with the terms of a
Project contract shall be subject to:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item A written notice of non-compliance with a 30-day cure period.
    \item Withholding of pending payment disbursements.
    \item Assessment of liquidated damages in the amount of 1\% of the
        contract value per day of non-compliance, up to a maximum of 15\%.
    \item Disqualification from future City contracts for a period of not
        fewer than three years.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Amendments}

This ordinance may be amended by a resolution of the Council approved by
a vote of not fewer than five of its seven members. Any proposed amendment
shall be subject to the same public review process described in
Section~3.2.
