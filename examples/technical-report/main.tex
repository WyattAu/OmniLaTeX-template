\chapter{Executive Summary}
This report presents the findings of a comprehensive performance audit
conducted on the organisation's core data-processing infrastructure.  Key
findings include a 38\% over-provisioning of compute resources, significant
I/O bottlenecks in the primary database tier, and an opportunity to reduce
annual infrastructure costs by approximately \$240\,000 through targeted
optimisations.

\chapter{Background}
The data-processing platform was originally deployed in 2019 to handle an
anticipated throughput of 10\,000 transactions per second.  Since then, organic
growth and three major product launches have increased peak load to
27\,000 TPS, exceeding the original design envelope by nearly a factor of
three.  This chapter describes the current architecture, its evolution, and the
symptoms that prompted the present audit.

\chapter{Recommendations}
Based on the analysis above, we recommend the following actions, prioritised by
impact and implementation effort:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Migrate to NVMe-backed storage} --- Expected to raise the
        IOPS ceiling to 80\,000, eliminating the current bottleneck.
    \item \textbf{Right-size compute instances} --- Reducing CPU and memory
        allocations by 30--35\% on 40\% of workloads without impacting SLAs.
    \item \textbf{Implement connection pooling} --- Reuse database connections
        to reduce per-query overhead by an estimated 15\%.
    \item \textbf{Introduce read replicas} --- Offload reporting queries to
        dedicated read replicas to isolate analytical workloads.
\end{enumerate}
