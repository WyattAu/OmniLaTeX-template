\section*{Chair's Message}

Dear Stakeholders,

The 2025 fiscal year marked a turning point for the Oakridge Community
Foundation. Amid shifting economic conditions, we deepened our commitment
to the communities we serve, expanded our programmatic reach, and
strengthened the governance structures that ensure accountability and
impact.

This year we distributed \$4.2 million in grants across education,
affordable housing, and public health---a 15\% increase over the prior
year. We welcomed seven new board members, completed a strategic planning
cycle that will guide our work through 2030, and launched two new
initiatives in workforce development and environmental resilience.

I am proud of what we have accomplished together, and I am confident that
the Foundation is well positioned to meet the challenges and opportunities
ahead. Thank you for your continued trust and partnership.

\vspace{1em}
\noindent\textbf{Margaret Liu}\\
Chair, Board of Directors

\section{Program Achievements}

\subsection{Education Initiative}

The Education Initiative distributed \$1.8 million in scholarships and
program grants during 2025:

\begin{itemize}
    \item 342 students received college scholarships, a 28\% increase
        over 2024.
    \item 15 schools received STEM laboratory equipment grants totaling
        \$420,000.
    \item The after-school tutoring program expanded to 8 new sites,
        serving an additional 600 students.
    \item Program evaluation showed a 12\% improvement in standardized
        test scores among participating students.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Affordable Housing}

The Foundation's housing programs produced measurable outcomes:

\begin{itemize}
    \item 28 new affordable housing units were completed in partnership
        with Habitat for Humanity.
    \item Emergency rental assistance prevented eviction for 194 families.
    \item Financial literacy workshops reached 820 participants, with 67\%
        reporting improved credit scores within six months.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Public Health}

Public health programs focused on underserved communities:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Mobile health clinics provided 4,200 patient visits across 12
        rural communities.
    \item Mental health support services expanded to include telehealth,
        reaching 380 individuals who previously lacked access.
    \item The community health worker training program graduated 45
        certified professionals.
\end{itemize}

\section{Goals for 2026}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Increase grant distribution to \$5 million} by expanding
        into workforce development and environmental resilience programs.
    \item \textbf{Launch the Community Investment Fund} with a target
        corpus of \$10 million to generate sustainable annual income for
        program funding.
    \item \textbf{Complete the digital transformation} of grant
        management systems, including online applications, automated
        reporting, and real-time impact dashboards.
    \item \textbf{Strengthen community engagement} by establishing a
        Community Advisory Council with 20 representatives from
        underserved neighborhoods.
    \item \textbf{Publish the Foundation's first equity audit} to assess
        the demographic distribution and impact of grant-making activities.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Committees}

The following standing committees met regularly throughout 2025:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Finance and Audit Committee} --- Oversaw annual audit,
        reviewed financial statements, and recommended investment policy
        changes.
    \item \textbf{Program Committee} --- Evaluated grant applications,
        monitored ongoing programs, and recommended funding allocations.
    \item \textbf{Governance and Nominating Committee} --- Recruited new
        board members, conducted board self-assessment, and updated
        bylaws.
    \item \textbf{Development Committee} --- Led fundraising campaigns,
        donor stewardship, and corporate partnership development.
\end{itemize}
