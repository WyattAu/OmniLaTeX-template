\section*{Abstract}
This thesis addresses the fundamental challenge of scaling Bayesian optimisation
(BO) to high-dimensional problems, where existing methods degrade sharply beyond
approximately 20 dimensions.  We propose a unified framework that combines
adaptive subspace identification, local surrogate modelling, and
information-theoretic acquisition functions to enable efficient optimisation in
dimensions ranging from 50 to 500.  Through extensive theoretical analysis and
empirical evaluation on both synthetic benchmarks and real-world engineering
problems, we demonstrate that our approach achieves up to 3.2$\times$ reduction
in simple regret compared to state-of-the-art methods.  The framework is made
available as an open-source Python library integrated with the BoTorch framework.
