\section{BACKGROUND AND CONTEXT}

\begin{enumerate}[resume]
    \item Atlantic Manufacturing Group, Inc. (``Atlantic'' or the ``Company'') is a mid-sized industrial manufacturing company specializing in precision-engineered components for the aerospace and defense industries.
    \item The Company operates three manufacturing facilities: (a) its headquarters and primary production facility in Buffalo, New York; (b) a secondary facility in Rochester, New York; and (c) a recently acquired facility in Erie, Pennsylvania.
    \item In connection with the Company's acquisition of the Erie facility from Precision Parts International, LLC (``Precision Parts'') on September 30, 2023, I was tasked with conducting a comprehensive financial due diligence review.
    \item As part of this review, I personally examined the financial records, operational data, and contractual obligations of Precision Parts for the three-year period ending December 31, 2022.
\end{enumerate}

\section{FINANCIAL FINDINGS}

\begin{enumerate}[resume]
    \item During my review of Precision Parts' financial statements, I identified significant discrepancies between the revenue figures reported in the Company's audited financial statements and the underlying transaction records maintained in its accounting system.

    Specifically, I found:
    \begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*)]
        \item Revenue recognition discrepancies totaling \$2,347,891 for fiscal year 2022, wherein revenue was recognized on contracts that had not yet achieved the performance obligations required under ASC 606;
        \item Unrecorded warranty liabilities of approximately \$1,125,000 relating to products delivered to Defense Systems Corp. under Contract No. DSC-2022-4471;
        \item Inventory valuation errors of \$876,432 due to the application of incorrect costing methodologies to the Erie facility's work-in-progress inventory.
    \end{enumerate}

    \item These discrepancies were material to the Company's assessment of the fair value of Precision Parts' assets and liabilities. Had I been aware of these discrepancies prior to closing, the purchase price would have been adjusted downward by approximately \$4,349,323, representing the aggregate impact of the identified items.

    \item I reported these findings to the Company's Board of Directors at a special meeting held on October 28, 2023. A copy of my report, dated October 25, 2023, is attached hereto as \textbf{Exhibit A} and incorporated herein by reference.

    \item Following my report, the Company engaged the independent accounting firm of Deloitte \& Touche LLP to conduct a forensic examination of Precision Parts' financial records. The preliminary findings of Deloitte \& Touche LLP, dated December 12, 2023, confirm the discrepancies I identified and indicate that additional irregularities may exist.
\end{enumerate}

\section{DUE DILIGENCE PROCEDURES}

\begin{enumerate}[resume]
    \item In conducting my financial due diligence, I performed the following procedures:
    \begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*)]
        \item Reviewed Precision Parts' audited financial statements for fiscal years 2020, 2021, and 2022;
        \item Examined general ledger entries, journal entries, and supporting documentation for all material transactions;
        \item Performed analytical procedures comparing reported financial results to industry benchmarks and prior period performance;
        \item Reviewed all material contracts, including customer agreements, supplier agreements, and lease obligations;
        \item Conducted interviews with Precision Parts' senior management team, including its former CFO, Robert J. Williams;
        \item Verified bank reconciliations and cash flow statements against third-party bank statements;
        \item Tested the completeness and accuracy of the fixed asset register against physical inventory counts.
    \end{enumerate}

    \item The procedures described above were performed in accordance with generally accepted auditing standards and the Company's standard due diligence protocol.

    \item I have over twenty-two (22) years of experience in financial management and accounting, including twelve (12) years in the manufacturing sector. I am a Certified Public Accountant licensed in the State of New York (License No. 089234).
\end{enumerate}

\section{ADDITIONAL MATTERS}

\begin{enumerate}[resume]
    \item As a direct result of the discrepancies identified, the Company has initiated legal proceedings against Precision Parts and its former management team in the United States District Court for the Western District of New York, Case No. 1:24-cv-00234.

    \item I have been designated as a witness in the pending litigation and expect to testify regarding the financial findings described herein.

    \item The Company has taken corrective action to remediate the identified issues, including:
    \begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*)]
        \item Restating its financial statements for the quarter ending December 31, 2023;
        \item Implementing enhanced internal controls over the Erie facility's accounting processes;
        \item Engaging additional accounting staff to oversee the integration of the Erie facility's financial reporting systems.
    \end{enumerate}

    \item The total cost of remediation, including legal fees, accounting fees, and internal resource allocation, is estimated to be approximately \$750,000.
\end{enumerate}
