\section{Problem Statement}

Unplanned equipment downtime costs the global manufacturing sector an estimated
\$50B annually. Traditional maintenance strategies---either reactive
(fix-on-failure) or preventive (time-based schedules)---fail to account for
the actual condition of equipment. This results in either costly unexpected
failures or wasteful premature part replacements.

Key pain points identified through interviews with 140+ plant managers:

\begin{itemize}
    \item 67\% report that unplanned downtime has increased over the past
          three years due to aging equipment and workforce shortages.
    \item Average cost of a single hour of unplanned downtime in automotive
          manufacturing: \$1.3M.
    \item 80\% of maintenance teams still rely on spreadsheets and manual
          inspections rather than data-driven systems.
    \item Existing IoT monitoring solutions generate overwhelming volumes of
          alert data without actionable insights, leading to alert fatigue.
\end{itemize}

\section{Solution}

NovaPulse's platform combines edge computing, machine learning, and a
cloud-based analytics dashboard to provide real-time health scoring and
remaining useful life (RUL) predictions for critical industrial assets.

\subsection{Core Technology}

\begin{description}
    \item[Edge Inference Engine.] Lightweight ML models deployed on-premise
        that process vibration, thermal, and acoustic sensor data in real time,
        operating within 50ms latency on standard industrial gateways.
    \item[Predictive Analytics Layer.] Cloud-hosted models trained on
        proprietary failure-mode datasets covering 14 equipment classes
        (pumps, compressors, turbines, conveyors, CNC machines, etc.).
    \item[Digital Twin Framework.] Physics-informed neural networks that
        create virtual representations of each asset, enabling ``what-if''
        scenario simulation for maintenance planning.
    \item[Prescriptive Recommendations.] Actionable work orders generated
        automatically, including part numbers, estimated labor hours, and
        optimal scheduling to minimize production impact.
\end{description}

\section{Risk Factors}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Technology risk.} ML model accuracy may degrade across
        different equipment types and operating conditions. Mitigation: continuous
        learning pipeline with human-in-the-loop validation; physics-informed
        constraints to ensure predictions remain within engineering bounds.
    \item \textbf{Market risk.} Economic downturn could reduce manufacturing
        capex and maintenance budgets. Mitigation: position as cost-reduction
        tool rather than discretionary investment; flexible pricing tiers.
    \item \textbf{Competitive risk.} Larger incumbents (Siemens, GE) could
        integrate predictive maintenance into existing platforms. Mitigation:
        deeper domain expertise, faster time-to-value, and multi-vendor
        platform independence.
    \item \textbf{Talent risk.} Competition for ML engineers and domain
        experts is intense. Mitigation: competitive compensation, meaningful
        equity grants, mission-driven culture, and remote-first policy for
        select roles.
\end{enumerate}
