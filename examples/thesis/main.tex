\chapter{Methodology}
Document experimental design, data acquisition, and analysis
steps~\cite{knuthTeXbook1986}.  The governing equation for the system under
study is given by
\begin{equation}
    \frac{\partial u}{\partial t}
    = \alpha \nabla^{2} u + f(\mathbf{x}, t),
    \label{eq:diffusion}
\end{equation}
where $u$ is the field variable, $\alpha$ is the diffusivity, and
$f(\mathbf{x}, t)$ represents the source term.

Numerical experiments were carried out on a uniform grid with spacing
$\Delta x = 0.01$ and a time step satisfying the CFL condition
$\Delta t \leq \Delta x^{2} / (2\alpha)$.  Convergence was verified by
refining the mesh until the $L^{2}$-norm of the error fell below $10^{-6}$.
