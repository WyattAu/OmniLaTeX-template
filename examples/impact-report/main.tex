\section{Program Highlights}

\subsection{Maternal and Child Health}

The Maternal and Child Health program operated across 8 countries, providing
prenatal care, skilled birth attendance, and postnatal support.

\begin{itemize}
    \item 142,000 pregnant women received at least four prenatal visits.
    \item Skilled birth attendance increased from 54\% to 72\% in target
          communities.
    \item Neonatal mortality decreased by 28\% across program sites.
    \item 45,000 children under five received full immunization series.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Water, Sanitation, and Hygiene (WASH)}

Our WASH program expanded to reach 380,000 additional people with clean
water access:

\begin{itemize}
    \item 260 new water points were constructed, serving 180,000 people.
    \item 1,840 household latrines were built through community-led
          total sanitation approaches.
    \item School handwashing stations were installed at 320 primary schools.
    \item Water quality testing showed 97\% compliance with WHO guidelines.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Infectious Disease Control}

\begin{itemize}
    \item 890,000 vaccinations administered across measles, polio, and
          rotavirus campaigns.
    \item 12,400 malaria cases were treated through community health worker
          networks.
    \item TB treatment adherence rate reached 91\%, up from 84\%.
    \item HIV testing reached 180,000 individuals, with 98\% linkage to care
          for positive results.
\end{itemize}

\section{Stories of Impact}

\subsection{Aisha's Story}

Aisha, a 28-year-old mother in rural Mali, walked three hours to reach the
nearest health facility during her first pregnancy. Thanks to our community
health worker program, a trained health agent was posted in her village.
When Aisha's second pregnancy developed complications, the health worker
facilitated an emergency referral that saved both mother and baby.

\subsection{Community Transformation in Bangladesh}

The village of Sundarpur had no clean water source within two kilometers.
Residents relied on a contaminated pond that caused frequent waterborne
illness. In 2025, our WASH program installed a deep tube well with a solar-powered
pump, serving 840 people. Diarrheal disease cases dropped by 62\% within
six months.

\section{Looking Ahead: 2026 Priorities}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Expand to 3 new countries} in Central Africa, targeting
          500,000 additional beneficiaries.
    \item \textbf{Launch digital health platform} for community health
          workers, enabling real-time data collection and telemedicine.
    \item \textbf{Scale WASH programs} to reach 2 million people with clean
          water by end of 2027.
    \item \textbf{Strengthen local capacity} by training 2,500 community
          health workers and establishing 50 community health committees.
    \item \textbf{Advocate for policy change} at national and international
          levels to increase primary healthcare funding.
\end{enumerate}
