\section{Executive Summary}

This feasibility study evaluates the technical, financial, and operational
viability of transitioning the Meridian Technologies campus from grid
electricity to a hybrid renewable energy system. The study examines three
distinct options and recommends Option B---a combined solar photovoltaic and
battery storage system---as the most cost-effective and sustainable path
forward.

The recommended system would reduce annual energy costs by 42\%, achieve
carbon neutrality for electricity by 2030, and pay for itself within 6.8
years. Total capital expenditure is estimated at \$4.2 million with an
annual operational savings of \$620,000.

\section{Background and Objectives}

\subsection{Current State}

The Meridian campus consists of four buildings totaling 280,000 square feet,
consuming approximately 3,200 MWh of electricity annually. Current grid
electricity costs average \$0.142/kWh, resulting in annual expenditure of
approximately \$454,000. The campus also consumes 12,000 therms of natural
gas for heating.

\subsection{Objectives}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item Reduce greenhouse gas emissions by at least 60\% within five years.
    \item Achieve energy cost savings of 35\% or more relative to current
          grid pricing.
    \item Ensure system reliability of 99.5\% or higher for critical loads.
    \item Complete installation without disrupting campus operations.
    \item Qualify for federal and state renewable energy incentives.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Recommendation}

Based on our analysis, we recommend \textbf{Option B: Solar PV + Battery
Storage}. This option provides the optimal balance of cost-effectiveness,
carbon reduction, and implementation feasibility.

\begin{itemize}
    \item The 6.8-year payback period falls well within the 10-year
          evaluation window.
    \item 82\% carbon reduction exceeds the 60\% target.
    \item System reliability of 99.7\% meets operational requirements.
    \item Net present value of \$2.1M demonstrates strong economic value.
    \item Qualification for \$1.2M in federal and state incentives
          significantly reduces net cost.
\end{itemize}

We recommend the Board authorize the \$4.2 million capital expenditure and
proceed with Phase 1 activities immediately.
